How it works

One pipeline, from solicitation to submission.

Every proposal follows the same six stages. RFPTrace structures your team's work around them — so nothing slips through and every decision is traceable.

01

Upload RFP

Drop in a PDF or Word solicitation — or paste a SAM.gov opportunity URL. RFPTrace parses the document and extracts every requirement, section, and deadline.

02

Compliance Matrix

Every shall, must, and requirement becomes a tracked line item. Assign owners, set status, and see at a glance what's addressed and what needs attention.

03

Document Library

Approved boilerplate, past proposals, and supporting docs live in one searchable library. Reuse what works without hunting through shared drives.

04

AI Drafting

Generate section drafts grounded in your document library and the solicitation's specific requirements. Your writers refine; RFPTrace handles the first pass.

05

Compliance Check

Before submission, verify every matrix line is addressed. Get a coverage report showing which requirements are met, marginal, or missing from your draft.

06

Export

Package your proposal for delivery. Export to Word, PDF, or your agency's required format — with a full audit trail of every decision your team made along the way.

Compliance Matrix

Every requirement, tracked from day one.

Government solicitations bury requirements across dozens of sections. RFPTrace extracts them automatically and builds a matrix that tracks status, ownership, and evidence — section by section.

  • Automatic extraction of "shall" and "must" language from solicitation documents
  • Assign requirements to individual contributors or teams
  • Real-time status tracking: addressed, in progress, or unresolved
  • FAR clause cross-reference built into every line item
  • Exportable matrix for agency submission or internal review
Proposal Intake

Go from solicitation to structured in minutes.

Drop in a PDF, Word document, or SAM.gov URL. RFPTrace parses the solicitation and organizes it into a structured workflow your team can act on immediately — no manual formatting required.

  • Supports PDF and Word solicitations up to 100 MB
  • SAM.gov opportunity import (coming soon)
  • FCI-only document handling — classified and defense-restricted content automatically rejected
  • Instant section breakdown: requirements, deliverables, evaluation criteria, deadlines
  • Company profile auto-populates cover pages (UEI, CAGE Code, NAICS, socioeconomic certs)

Built-in extensions

The tools government contractors actually need.

RFPTrace ships with purpose-built tools for the unique demands of government contracting — not generic project management features retrofitted to proposals.

FAR Clause Library

Full Federal Acquisition Regulation clause library, searchable and cross-referenced with your proposal requirements. Stay current without manual updates.

SAM.gov Monitor

Track opportunities matching your NAICS codes. Get notified when new solicitations post, amendments drop, or deadlines shift — without refreshing SAM.gov manually.

Amendment Diff

When a solicitation is amended, see exactly what changed — highlighted side by side. Know immediately whether your draft needs updating and where.

Bid / No-Bid

Structured scoring for bid decisions. Evaluate win probability, resource fit, competitive landscape, and strategic alignment before committing your team's time.

Team collaboration

Built for proposal teams, not solo writers.

Government proposals are a team sport. RFPTrace gives every contributor — writers, volume leads, reviewers, pricing — a structured workspace and a clear view of what's theirs.

Pink Team and Red Team reviews have dedicated workflows. Analytics show which sections are ahead, behind, or stalled — so your proposal manager doesn't have to chase status by email.

Team Members
Role-based access for writers, reviewers, and admins. Contributors only see what they're assigned.
Pink Team Review
Structured early review workflow with annotated feedback tied to specific proposal sections.
Proposal Analytics
Section completion rates, contributor activity, and time-to-draft — all visible to proposal managers.
Notifications
Deadline alerts, review assignments, and amendment notices — delivered when they matter.
